St. Paul suburb Rosemount has a population of 24,292 people. There are many great places to live in Rosemount, a suburb of Minneapolis, which is located in Dakota County. Rosemount's sparse suburban population and high homeownership rate make it a desirable place to call home for many people. There are numerous parks to choose from in Rosemount. Rosemount is home to many family members and has a conservative leaning population. Rosemount's public schools are highly regarded.

To sum it up, the town is a great place to settle down and raise a family.

Rosemount was one of the 3,625 U.S. towns with a population of 10,000 to 50,000 that were evaluated using statistics, a site visit, and in-depth interviews with residents for the "Best Places to Live 2015" list.

The magazine didn't give the neighborhood its top rating. "Best Places to be a Kid" ranked Rosemount fourth on the list. There are only five towns listed in Kid's list.

Rosemount places a high value on education. Award-winning public and private schools offer exceptional access to education for both children and adults. Independent School District 196, one of the largest districts in the state, covers a large portion of the city. For those who prefer to attend a non-public school, there are twelve options. Rosemount is also home to Dakota County Technical College, one of the state's largest technical colleges.

A family-friendly community, it has single-family residences, townhomes and condominiums as well as senior living and low-income housing, with an average monthly rental price of $1,200.

Community of Entrepreneurs

Rosemount has a thriving, cohesive business community thanks to the efforts of local businesses, the city government, the chamber of commerce, and other civic groups. Rosemount has set aside almost 500 acres of industrial and commercially designated property for future development. Rosemount's economic community has access to a wide range of transportation options, including rail, air, barge, and motorway. Rosemount is connected to Minneapolis and St. Paul by four main freeways.

Flint Hills Resources (previously Koch Refinery), SKB (industrial waste containment plant), Spectro Alloys, and Hawkins Chemical are among the companies located in the northeastern section of the city. Greif Brothers (multiwall bags) and the National Guard regional headquarters are located in the city's west. Also in 1995, Geometrix and Proto Labs moved into the city's 80-acre business park.
